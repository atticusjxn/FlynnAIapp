## Post 1 — the pivot, told straight (founder voice)

Most "AI for tradies" pitches lead with the app. We're leading with the phone call, because
that's where the job is actually won or lost.

A tradie up a ladder can't answer. The caller doesn't leave a voicemail — they call the next
name on the list. That's the moment we built Flynn around: something that picks up, sounds like
an actual person, and books the job before the caller's even thought about ringing someone else.

Everything after that — the invoice with the job photos on it, the pay link, the chasing until
it's paid — was already true of Flynn. The receptionist is the front door we were missing.

## Post 2 — the problem, in one stat-shaped line

Missed calls aren't lost admin. They're lost jobs.

Every trade business has felt this: the phone rings while you're mid-job, you can't get to it in
time, and the caller doesn't wait around — they ring the next name on the list. Voicemail
doesn't fix this. Most people don't leave one.

We built Flynn to answer instead. It sounds like a real person, works out what the job is, and
books it in before you've even seen the missed call notification. Then it handles the invoice
and the chasing too.

Would love feedback from anyone running a trade business on what "good" looks like for a
receptionist that isn't human.

## Post 3 — product detail, for a technical/builder audience

One design choice worth sharing: Flynn's receptionist never uses a "press 1 for bookings" IVR
menu. It's a real conversation, start to finish.

The reasoning: a caller who hits a menu already suspects they're talking to a machine, and trust
drops immediately for a tradie's own customers. A conversation that actually listens, asks a
sensible follow-up question, and books a real time slot reads completely differently — even
though it's the same AI doing the work underneath.

Small decision, but it's the one that decides whether this feels like a demo or like the
business's actual receptionist.

## Post 4 — the money loop (payments/ops angle)

The unglamorous truth about small trade businesses: most of the "admin" that eats a week isn't
scheduling, it's chasing money that's already owed.

Flynn's loop after a job: photo invoice with the before/afters on it, a pay link, and automatic
follow-ups on anything that goes quiet. The receptionist gets the job booked; this is what
actually gets the tradie paid on time.

We think this pairing — answer the call, then don't let the invoice go cold — is the part that
makes the whole thing worth paying for, not just the novelty of an AI voice.

## Post 5 — short, conversational, for engagement

Genuine question for anyone in trades or small-business services: when you miss a call, what
actually happens next? Do you call back same day? Does it just... not happen?

We're building the answer to "you never have to find out" — an AI receptionist that picks up
when you can't — and the honest answers to this question have shaped more of the product than
any spec doc.

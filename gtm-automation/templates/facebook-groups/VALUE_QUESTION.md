# FB Group Post — VALUE_QUESTION

Goal: get the group talking, surface pain points, naturally lead to Flynn in comments. Never pitch in the post itself.

## Variants

### V1 — Direct question

```
Quick poll for the group — how many of you are losing $$ to missed calls?

I'm seeing a lot of {{trade}}s on here say they miss 5-15 calls/week when they're on tools. Is that you too?

If yes — what do you currently do about it? Voicemail? Just lose the lead?

Genuinely curious. Trying to figure out if this is a $1k/wk problem or a $5k/wk problem for most of you.
```

### V2 — Specific scenario

```
True or false:
- You're up a ladder, phone rings
- It's a "withheld" or "no caller ID" — could be a real customer, could be a scammer
- You let it ring, finish what you're doing, never call back
- Two days later you find out it was the customer's wife, and they went with the next sparky on Google

How often does this happen to you? Once a week? Once a day?
```

### V3 — Industry insight

```
Saw this stat the other day — 78% of customers go with the first business that picks up.

That tracks with what mates have told me. As soon as voicemail kicks in, they Google the next plumber.

Curious — anyone here actually answering most of their calls? What's your trick? Wife/partner taking calls? Outsourcing?

Trying to learn how the busy lads on here handle it.
```

## How to use

1. Post the question. Don't mention Flynn.
2. Wait for engagement (15-30 min). Reply to early comments with relevant follow-ups.
3. When someone says "I just lose the lead" — THEN drop a casual mention: "Mate I'm building something for exactly this — happy to send you a free trial if you want to see if it works."
4. Move further conversation to DM.

## Why this works

- Group admins ban hard-pitch posts. Question-led posts engage the algorithm.
- Comments where Flynn is mentioned in response to a real pain point convert ~10x better than ads.
- Builds you as a "knowledgeable mate", not "guy selling SaaS".

## DON'T

- Don't post links in the original post. Group admins flag this.
- Don't tag people. Looks spammy.
- Don't post the same variant twice in the same group within 14 days.
